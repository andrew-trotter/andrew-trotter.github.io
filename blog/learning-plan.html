Learning Plan

Bootcamp Learning Plan

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

My heart has always been driven towards helping people. I've considered many paths like Physiotherapy, Psychotherapy or a military organisation. I've also always had a head for technology. I've enjoyed my fair share of video games, built computers, operated digital printers and helped friends and family with technology qualms and queries. I used to write blog posts as a young man finding his way in the world and the internet has always been a place I come to for help or guidance.

My long term goal is to be in the tech industry and use that to help people. I will begin as a web developer as we will always need an interface to the internet, but will keep my eyes open to what draws me. It's ambiguous and doesn't have a destination and that's awesome, because technology is always changing and so can I.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

My views on my strengths and weaknesses haven't changed. I have an improved understanding of them and I will continue to improve that understanding into bootcamp. I will, for example, still have a desire to get unstuck myself, but I'll be better at involving people earlier on so the project can move more smoothly. I will be curious and go down rabbit holes, but I'll be more mindful of time commitments and whether I can afford that rabbit hole or not.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

I feel pretty good overall for non-technical issues. I have some concern for the workload as I live a bit far from campus, but I have support at home and I am capable. I will be living nearby house-sitting my brother's place which will be helpful. So I still feel overall good about this too.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

Being quite socially aware, I care about what my peers think of me and not wanting to disappoint them. This tends to make me conflict-avoidant so this is something I'd really appreciate improving this aspect of communciation at bootcamp if given the opportunity. This would tie into giving or receiving feedback and handling disagreements.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

My experience from the support from the Foundations team has been amazing so far. A great breadth of technical and non-technical knowledge and support. I only hope that this continues with the bootcamp team.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

I expect myself to ask for help if I find myself stuck with both technical and non-technical problems. I understand asking for help is a general challenge for me, but I trust those I work with at bootcamp, peers and the team, are willing to support each other as I would be willing to support them too. I understand that this is a place of growth and to grow I must share that which needs to grow.

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

My long term goal is use technology to create or assist in creating cool tools or experiences for people that help them. That could be through creating it myself or in a team, managing and developing a team, or teaching people to build tools. I'll start with learning web development to enter the industry, then navigate towards what draws my attention. Long term I could move into other sectors like DevOps or Security.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning.

    Strengths:
  • I can be very focussed
  • I enjoy helping others
  • I enjoy exploring
    Limitations:
  • I can get bored and find it difficult to focus
  • If I'm stuck I can find it difficult to find a balance between asking for help and trying to figure it out myself
  • I can be disorganised or unstructured in my learning. Especially if I go down a rabbit hole or get hung up on something unimportant.

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?


Receiving feedback (positive or negative) and navigating conflicts. I am very conscientious and care a lot what people think of me or my work. Because of this, I tend to avoid (what I consider) potential conflicts. Organising my time more effectively.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include:

  • An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, Facilitators and industry/community representatives
  • What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you

I commit to behaving in a professional manner and will aim to not derail the productivity of my peers. I will treat others with respect, patience and kindness. I will promote a cooperative and encouraging learning environment to work will with others. To manage my workload I will use commit the time necessary and use time-blocks as a guideline. If I find the workload is getting too much, and help from peers isn't adequate, I will contact a facilitator to help me make a plan for how to prioritise and manage it more effectively. I will be open to this being a logistical/organisational or personal/emotional resolution.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

If I find myself getting distracted or too focussed on something, I will try to make a note of it for later reading. I will participate fully throughout the course and share if I have questions, answers or concerns. I will support others as best I can.

A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team:

I expect the facilitation team to be professional, respectful, kind and reasonable. I expect them to foster an encouraging and supportive learning environment.

Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things. For example, you might have whanau commitments like school drop-offs. It's important to plan these in.
